Benefits of DevOps

  • Rapid Product Delivery

    Fast product delivery allows developers to deliver products to customers faster and to become more efficient in delivering high-value results. The use of microservices and continuous delivery lets teams take ownership of services and make releases faster.

  • Reliability

    The use of practices like Continuous Integration and Delivery to test each change makes the system functional and safe. This allows teams to reliably deliver at a more rapid pace while maintaining a positive experience for end users.

  • Greater Scalability

    Devops allows for management of infrastructure and development processes at scale. Automation and consistency help in managing complex and changing systems efficiently and with reduced risk

  • Collaboration

    The DevOps culture emphasizes on ownership and accountability. Developers and Operations teams collaborate closely and combine workflows to deliver a reliable system. This reduces inefficiencies and saves time.

  • Faster issue resolution and reduced complexity

Challenges of DevOps

  • Too much focus on tools

    Many companies pay too much attention to tools and not to culture, collaboration and communication. This leads to a complex and counterproductive environment

  • Toolset Conflicts

    Development and operation teams make use of different tools, and integrating these tools can be problematic and can create problems and cause deployments to take longer than expected.

  • Continuous Learning

    Devops involves people learning at all times because software is constantly changing and new tools and technologies are constantly being developed. Getting started with continous learning can be one of the biggest issues for organizations.

  • Implementing a Test automation Strategy

    Often times, companies prioritize CI/CD rollouts over test automation. Testing is important for Devops implementation to be fruitful. Testing automation improves and speeds up the process of developing quality software
